The Sudmant Lab @ UC Berkeley
research
genomics and evolution of cellular and organismal diversity, aging, and stress

The Sudmant Lab at UC Berkeley uses computational, statistical, and experimental methods to interrogate genetic and molecular phenotypic diversity at both the organismal and cellular level. We are particularly interested in studying the causes and consequences of stress and aging in organisms with a focus on post-transcriptional gene regulation, somatic mutation, and genetic adaption in long (and short) lived species. Other topics of interest include population genetics and human diveristy of copy number variation.

How is the fidelity of post-transcriptional gene regulatory processes impacted by stress and age?
How do individual cell types differently accumulate age-associated DNA damage and structural variants?
How are gene expression networks reshaped in healthy aging and age associated neurodegenerative disease?
What evolutionary processes and genetic signatures underlie differences in the longevity of closely related species?
